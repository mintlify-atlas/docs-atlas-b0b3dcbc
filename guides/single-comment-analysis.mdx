## Overview

The Single Comment Analysis feature allows you to quickly analyze the sentiment of individual text comments. SentimentAPI's AI model will classify the comment as either positive or negative, along with a confidence score.

## Accessing the Analysis Dashboard

After logging in, you'll be automatically redirected to the **Panel de Análisis** (Analysis Dashboard), where you can analyze individual comments.

## Analyzing a Comment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Your Comment">
    In the text input field, type or paste the comment you want to analyze. The placeholder text reads:
    
    ```
    Escribe comentarios (uno por línea para análisis masivo)...
    ```
    
    For single comment analysis, simply enter one comment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit for Analysis">
    Click the **"Analizar Sentimientos"** (Analyze Sentiments) button. The button will change to "Procesando..." (Processing) while the AI model analyzes your comment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Results">
    Once processing is complete, a results table will appear below the input form showing the sentiment analysis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the Results Table

The results are displayed in a comprehensive table with the following columns:

### 1. Comentario (Comment)

Displays the original text you submitted for analysis. Long comments may be truncated in the display.

### 2. Prevision (Prediction)

Shows the AI model's sentiment classification:

- **POSITIVO** - Displayed with a green badge and smile icon 😊
- **NEGATIVO** - Displayed with a red badge and frown icon ☹️

The color coding makes it easy to quickly identify the sentiment at a glance.

### 3. Probabilidad (Probability/Confidence)

This column shows two important pieces of information:

- **Percentage score**: The model's confidence in its prediction (e.g., 87.3%)
- **Visual progress bar**: A color-coded bar representing the confidence level
  - Green bar for positive sentiments
  - Red bar for negative sentiments

<Note>
  Higher probability scores (closer to 100%) indicate greater confidence in the prediction. Scores near 50% suggest the model is less certain about the sentiment.
</Note>

## Interpreting Confidence Scores

### High Confidence (80-100%)

The model is very confident in its classification. These predictions are typically reliable.

**Example**: A comment like "This product is absolutely amazing!" might receive:
- Prevision: POSITIVO
- Probabilidad: 95.8%

### Medium Confidence (60-80%)

The model has moderate confidence. The sentiment is likely correct, but there may be some ambiguity in the text.

**Example**: "It's okay, nothing special" might receive:
- Prevision: NEGATIVO
- Probabilidad: 72.1%

### Low Confidence (50-60%)

The model is uncertain. The comment may contain mixed sentiments or neutral language.

<Tip>
  For low confidence predictions, consider reviewing the comment manually to understand the nuanced sentiment.
</Tip>

## Example Workflow

Here's a complete example of analyzing a comment:

1. **Input**: "The customer service was excellent and very helpful!"
2. **Click**: "Analizar Sentimientos"
3. **Result**:
   - Comentario: "The customer service was excellent and very helpful!"
   - Prevision: POSITIVO (green badge with smile icon)
   - Probabilidad: 92.4% (green progress bar)

## Tips for Best Results

- **Use clear, complete sentences**: The AI model works best with well-formed text
- **Avoid mixed sentiments in one comment**: For analysis of complex feedback, consider breaking it into separate comments
- **Language consistency**: Ensure your comments are in the language the model was trained on
