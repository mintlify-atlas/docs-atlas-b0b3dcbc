## Overview

SentimentAPI uses secure session-based authentication to protect your data. Once you log in, you'll receive a session token that allows you to access all features of the platform.

## Logging In

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Login Page">
    Open your browser and go to the SentimentAPI login page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Your Credentials">
    Provide your registered email address and password in the login form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit the Form">
    Click the login button to authenticate. If your credentials are correct, you'll be automatically redirected to the sentiment analysis dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Session Management

### How Sessions Work

When you successfully log in, SentimentAPI creates a secure session token that is stored in your browser as an **httpOnly cookie**. This means:

- The token is automatically sent with every request you make
- It cannot be accessed by JavaScript, providing enhanced security
- You don't need to manually manage or include the token in your requests

### Session Duration

<Note>
  Your session token is valid for **2 hours** from the time you log in.
</Note>

After 2 hours of inactivity, your session will expire and you'll need to log in again.

## When Your Session Expires

If you try to access a protected feature after your session has expired, you'll see a message indicating that you're not authenticated:

<Warning>
  **No autenticado**
  
  Your session has expired or you haven't logged in. Please log in again.
</Warning>

When you see this message:

1. Click the **"Ir al Login"** (Go to Login) button
2. Re-enter your credentials on the login page
3. You'll be redirected back to the application

## Troubleshooting Login Issues

### Incorrect Credentials

If you enter the wrong email or password, you'll see an error message:

```
Credenciales incorrectas. Intenta de nuevo.
```

Double-check your email and password, then try again.

### Service Unavailable

If the authentication service is temporarily down, you'll see:

```
El servicio de autenticación no está disponible.
```

<Tip>
  Wait a few moments and try logging in again. If the problem persists, contact support.
</Tip>

### Invalid Token Response

In rare cases, you might see:

```
No se recibió un token válido.
```

This indicates a server-side issue. Try logging in again, and contact support if the problem continues.

## Security Best Practices

- **Never share your credentials** with anyone
- **Log out** when you're done using the platform, especially on shared computers
- Use a **strong, unique password** for your SentimentAPI account
- If you notice any suspicious activity, change your password immediately
